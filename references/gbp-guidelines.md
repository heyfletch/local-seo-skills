# Google Business Profile Guidelines

## 1. Business Eligibility

### REQUIRED — To qualify for a Business Profile:
- The business MUST make in-person contact with customers during its stated hours.
- The business MUST either have a physical location customers can visit OR travel to customers at their locations.

### ALLOWED — Exceptions that ARE eligible:
- ATMs, video-rental kiosks, and express mail drop boxes (MUST include contact information for customer help).
- Seasonal businesses (e.g., an ice-skating rink only open in winter) — MUST display permanent signage at their location year-round.
- Delivery-only food services — with specific conditions (see Virtual Food Brands section).

### NOT ALLOWED — The following are NOT eligible for a Business Profile:
- Rental or for-sale properties (vacation homes, model homes, vacant apartments). Exception: Sales or leasing offices ARE eligible.
- An ongoing service, class, or meeting at a location the business does not own or have authority to represent.
- Lead generation agents or companies.
- Brands, organizations, artists, and other online-only businesses.
- Businesses associated with age-restricted products/services (alcohol, cannabis, weapons) that operate as service-area businesses without a storefront.
- Businesses whose address is a P.O. box or mailbox at a remote location.
- Businesses that do not exist at the claimed location (Google will disable the profile).

---

## 2. Ownership and Authorization

### REQUIRED:
- Only business owners or authorized representatives may verify and manage a Business Profile.
- Management access can be shared by adding a manager.

### Ownership may be revoked if:
- The owner has not signed in to their account recently.
- The business confirmed the owner/agency is no longer authorized.
- Google has been unable to reach the owner by email after multiple attempts.
- The profile owner deleted the profile.
- The profile was created through another Google product and that product profile has been suspended.

### Authorized Representatives MUST:
- NEVER claim a Business Profile without express consent from the business owner.
- NEVER make false, misleading, or unrealistic claims.
- NEVER use harassing, abusive, or untrustworthy tactics with potential or existing customers.
- ALWAYS work directly with the business owner to complete verification.
- ALWAYS ensure the business owner understands what a Business Profile is and where the data is used.
- ALWAYS keep the business owner informed about actions taken on the profile.
- ALWAYS respond to management access requests promptly.
- ALWAYS transfer Business Profile ownership to the business owner immediately upon request.
- Whenever possible, encourage the business owner to create an account, own the profile, and add the representative as a manager (not owner).

### REQUIRED for phone number and website:
- The phone number and website for a Business Profile MUST always be the single, authoritative phone number and website for the business.
- MUST be verifiable by the business owner.
- Website content MUST be owned and managed by the business owner.

## 3. Business Name Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- The business name MUST reflect the business's real-world name as used consistently on its storefront, website, stationery, and as known to customers.
- Additional details (address, service area, business hours, category) MUST go in other sections — NOT in the name.

### NOT ALLOWED in the business name:
- Marketing taglines (e.g., "America's Most Convenient Bank").
- Store codes (e.g., "- 2872").
- Trademark/registered signs (e.g., (R)).
- Fully capitalized words UNLESS that is the real brand style (allowed: SUBWAY; exceptions: KFC, IHOP, JCPenney are acceptable as-is).
- Business hours information (e.g., "Open 24 hours", "(Closed)").
- Phone numbers or website URLs.
- Special characters (%$@/") or irrelevant legal terms (LLC, LTD, INC) — UNLESS real-world proof (signage, business cards, invoices) consistently shows them as part of the name. Allowed examples: H&M, T.J.Maxx, Re/Max.
- Service or product information (e.g., "4G LTE", "Auto Service Experts").
- Location information beyond the city/neighborhood name (e.g., "I-93 at Exit 2" is not allowed, but "Salem" or "SOHO" is allowed).
- Containment information (e.g., "in Duane Reade", "at Stanford Shopping Center", "inside Best Buy").

### ALLOWED:
- The actual, real-world business name.
- Special characters or legal terms IF backed by real-world proof of consistent use.
- Titles or degree certifications for individual practitioners (e.g., Dr., MD, JD, Esq., CFA).

---

## 4. Address Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- Use a precise, accurate address and/or service area.
- The address MUST be the actual, real-world location of the business.
- Suite numbers, floors, and building numbers may be included.
- Cross-streets and nearby landmarks SHOULD only be included where the official street address doesn't accurately pinpoint the location.
- If specifying a mailbox or suite number: physical address goes in "Address Line 1," mailbox/suite in "Address Line 2."
- Businesses showing their address on Google MUST maintain permanent fixed signage of their business name at the address.
- If the address doesn't have a street number or the system can't find it, the business location can be pinned directly on the map.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- P.O. boxes or mailboxes at remote locations.
- Virtual office addresses (renting a physical mailing address but not operating there).
- Co-working space offices UNLESS the office maintains clear signage, receives customers during business hours, and is staffed by the business's own staff during business hours.
- URLs, keywords, or other non-address information in address lines.
- Creating more than one profile per business location (in a single account or multiple accounts). Exception: Individual practitioners and departments may have separate profiles.

---

## 5. Service-Area Business Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- Service-area businesses SHOULD have one profile for the central office or location with a designated service area.
- Service-area businesses SHOULD hide their business address from customers (e.g., plumbers operating from a residential address).
- The service area boundary SHOULD NOT extend farther than about 2 hours of driving time from where the business is based (larger areas may be appropriate for some businesses).
- If the business has different service locations with separate service areas and separate staff, one profile per location is allowed.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Service-area businesses CANNOT list a "virtual" office unless it is staffed during business hours.
- Businesses with age-restricted products/services (alcohol, cannabis, weapons) are NOT permitted as service-area businesses without a storefront.

### Hybrid businesses (e.g., auto repair shop with garage AND roadside service):
- CAN show their storefront address AND designate a service area.
- The business location MUST be staffed and able to receive customers during stated hours.

### Storefront vs. Service-Area:
- No storefront with clear signage but travels to customers: ONE service-area profile allowed.
- Different locations with separate service areas and staff: one profile per location.

---

## 6. Website and Phone Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- Provide a phone number that connects to the individual business location.
- Provide a website that represents the individual business location.
- Use a local phone number instead of a central call center helpline whenever possible.
- The phone number MUST be under the direct control of the business.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Phone numbers or URLs that redirect or "refer" users to landing pages or phone numbers other than those of the actual business (including social media pages).
- Premium phone numbers (regardless of rate charged to the caller).

### ALLOWED:
- Additional phone numbers can be used on Google Business Profile websites and other local surfaces.

---

## 7. Business Hours Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- Provide regular customer-facing hours of operation.
- Current seasonal hours may be used as regular hours if applicable.
- Special hours may be set for particular days (holidays, special events).
- Departments MUST list their own business hours on their separate profile; the main business lists its own hours on the main profile.

### Businesses that SHOULD NOT provide hours:
- Indoor lodging (hotels, motels).
- Schools and universities.
- Movie theaters.
- Transportation services and airports.
- Event venues and natural features.
- Businesses with varied hours (different schedules for activities, showtimes, worship services, classes).
- Businesses that operate only by appointment.

### Industry-specific hour rules:
- **Banks:** Use lobby hours; if unavailable, use drive-through hours. ATMs attached to banks can have their own separate profile with different hours.
- **Car dealerships:** Use car sales hours. If new and pre-owned differ, use new sales hours.
- **Gas stations:** Use gas pump hours.
- **Restaurants:** Use dine-in hours. If not available: takeout hours, then drive-through hours, then delivery hours (in that priority order).
- **Storage facilities:** Use office hours; if unavailable, use front gate hours.

### "More hours" feature:
- Can be used to highlight times for specific services.
- SHOULD generally be a subset of primary hours.

### Seasonal hours:
- During open season: set regular hours; may add special hours for holidays/closures.
- During off-season: mark business as temporarily closed.
- Set regular hours again when the business reopens.
- May indicate seasonal operation in the business description.

---

## 8. Category Guidelines

### REQUIRED:
- Use as few categories as possible to describe the overall core business.
- Choose categories that are as specific as possible but representative of the main business.
- Select categories that complete the statement: "This business IS a" (not "this business HAS a").
- Focus on the most specific categories; Google implicitly includes more general parent categories.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Using categories solely as keywords or to describe attributes.
- Using categories that pertain to other nearby or related businesses (e.g., a business physically inside yours, or the entity that contains your business).

### Co-located businesses:
- If your business contains another business you don't own/operate, only use categories for YOUR business.
  - Example: Barnes & Noble should NOT add "Coffee Shop" because Starbucks operates inside it.
- Co-located businesses that need both categories SHOULD create two separate profiles with different names (see Departments section).
  - Restaurant/Cafe/Bar inside a Hotel/Motel.
  - Pharmacy inside a Supermarket/Grocery Store.
  - Gas Station next to a Supermarket/Grocery Store.
  - Car Rental at an Airport (only if pick-up and drop-off are at separate locations).

---

## 9. Business Description Guidelines

### REQUIRED / RECOMMENDED:
- Use the description to provide useful information on services, products, mission, and history.
- Be upfront and honest.
- Focus on content that is relevant and useful to customers.

### NOT ALLOWED in descriptions:
- Low-quality, irrelevant, or distracting content (misspellings, gimmicky characters, gibberish).
- Special promotions, prices, or offer sales (e.g., "Everything on sale, -50%", "Best bagels in town for $5!").
- Links of any type.
- Content irrelevant to the business or with no clear association to it.

---

## 10. Content Guidelines (General)

### REQUIRED:
- All content MUST accurately represent the business/location.
- Published content SHOULD highlight what makes the business unique.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Private or confidential information (personal financial info, government-issued IDs, contact info linked to a name, sensitive records, images/transcripts/links containing personal info).
- Solicitation of personal or confidential information from others.

### ALLOWED:
- Merchants MAY post their own business contact information (social media handles, email, phone number) on their own profile or in response to reviews, Q&A, etc.

---

## 11. Photos, Videos, and Posts Policy

### REQUIRED:
- All submitted photos, videos, and posts MUST comply with Google's prohibited and restricted content policies.
- You are responsible for ensuring posted imagery complies with all applicable laws and regulations.

### NOT ALLOWED in posts:
- Phone numbers in post content (this is called "phone stuffing"). Instead, attach a "Call now" button using the verified Business Profile phone number.

### Hotel-specific post rules:
- Hotels CAN create local posts for relevant, timely info.
- Hotels CANNOT create "offer" posts or any post mentioning/linking to deals, promotions, special offers, or discounts.

---

## 12. Business Links Policies

### REQUIRED:
- Business links MUST lead to a dedicated landing page for the specific business/location.
- For multi-location businesses, links MUST lead to a website for the specific location (not a general landing page or another location's page).
- Business links MUST allow customers to complete the designated action (e.g., an "order" link must allow order completion).
- The business MUST be verified before adding links.
- Links MUST be functional (return HTTP 200 OK status), fully loadable (including all resources), and not geoblocked.
- Links MUST be accessible to Google's automated crawlers (User Agent: Google-BusinessLinkVerification) without restriction.
- Maximum of 20 links per transaction type.

### NOT ALLOWED for business links:
- Social media sites.
- Messaging links.
- App store links.
- Link shorteners.
- Links containing spaces, symbols, backslashes, or invalid protocols.
- Duplicate links (same URL or same domain already in use).
- Blocking bot traffic, rate limiting, requiring CAPTCHAs/login for crawlers, IP blocking, or content cloaking against Google's verification crawlers.

### IMPORTANT:
- Google's business link verification crawlers do NOT follow robots.txt rules.
- Google will regularly check links for compliance and may remove non-compliant links.

---

## 13. Menu Guidelines

### Two types of menus:
1. **Eating/drinking establishment menus** — complete set of food and drink items available.
2. **Service business menus** — complete set of services available (e.g., barber, spa, car repair).

### REQUIRED:
- The menu MUST be representative of items/services actually available to customers.
- Full menus can be meal-specific and have links to other menu pages.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Sample menus listing only "popular items" or similar excerpts.
- Menu URLs that are direct links to third-party ordering or delivery services.
- Third parties managing profiles MUST notify and have consent from the business owner before submitting a menu URL.

---

## 14. Product Guidelines

### Eligibility:
- Available for retail businesses in eligible countries (US, CA, UK, IE).
- Products can be added via Product Editor or by connecting a Point of Sale system to the Local Inventory app.

### REQUIRED:
- All products MUST adhere to Google's Shopping Ads Policy.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Content related to regulated products/services: alcohol, tobacco, gambling, financial services, pharmaceuticals and unapproved supplements, health/medical devices.

### Consequences:
- Violating product policies may result in removal of the ENTIRE product catalog (including compliant products).
- If the Business Profile is suspended, all products gain "Not Approved" status.
- Products are automatically reprocessed within 5 days of profile reinstatement.

---

## 15. Chains, Brands, and Multi-Location Guidelines

### Name consistency (REQUIRED):
- All locations of a business within the same country MUST have the same name.
- Exceptions:
  - Multiple types of businesses, sub-brands, or departments may use distinct names IF consistently applied across all locations of that type (e.g., "Walmart Supercenter" vs. "Walmart Express").
  - Locations that consistently use a different name on storefront/website/stationery may use that different name (e.g., "PFK" in Quebec vs. "KFC" elsewhere).

### Category consistency (REQUIRED):
- All locations MUST share the one category that best represents the business.
- This applies within sub-groups (e.g., all "Gap Kids" have "Children's Clothing Store").
- Some locations may have additional categories beyond the shared one.

### Two or more brands at the same location:
- MUST NOT combine brand names into a single profile (e.g., NOT "KFC / Taco Bell").
- Pick one brand's name per profile. If brands operate independently, use separate profiles.
- If a business sells another brand's product: use only the selling business's name, UNLESS it is an authorized, fully dedicated seller/franchisee of that brand.

---

## 16. Departments Within Organizations

### ALLOWED to have their own profile:
- Departments within businesses, universities, hospitals, and government institutions that are publicly-facing and operate as distinct entities.
- MUST have a separate customer entrance.
- MUST have a distinct name different from the main business and other departments.
- MUST have a most-representative category different from the main business and other departments.
- May have different hours from the main business.

### NOT ALLOWED to have their own profile:
- Sub-sections that are not distinct entities (e.g., the Apple products section of Best Buy, the hot food bar inside Whole Foods).

---

## 17. Individual Practitioners

### Definition:
- A public-facing professional with their own customer base (doctors, dentists, lawyers, financial planners, insurance/real estate agents, etc.).

### ALLOWED to create a dedicated Business Profile if:
- They operate in a public-facing role.
- They can be contacted directly at the verified location during stated hours.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Support staff creating their own profiles.
- A practitioner having multiple profiles for different specializations.
- Sales associates or lead generation agents for corporations.

### Multiple practitioners at one location:
- The organization SHOULD create a profile for the location, separate from practitioner profiles.
- The practitioner's profile title SHOULD include only the practitioner's name (NOT the organization name).

### Solo practitioners at branded organizations:
- Share a single profile with the organization.
- Name format: "[brand/company]: [practitioner name]" (e.g., "Allstate: Joe Miller").

---

## 18. Virtual Food Brands

### Co-located food brands offering pick-up:
- MUST have permanent separate signage.
- SHOULD display address only if they offer pick-up to all customers.
- Delivery-only brands at shared kitchens MUST hide their address and add service areas.

### Delivery-only food brands:
- ALLOWED if they have distinct branded packaging AND a distinct website.
- Multiple virtual brands at one location are allowed but subject to additional verification.
- MUST add service areas and hide the address.
- If a food brand authorizes a virtual kitchen as a verified provider, the kitchen may manage the brand's profile once authorization is confirmed.
- The facility housing delivery-only brands (e.g., DoorDash Kitchens) MAY have its own separate profile, claimable only by someone affiliated with the facility.

---

## 19. Rebranding Guidelines

### Eligible for rebranding (name change without creating a new profile):
- Minor name change where proper nouns and services remain unchanged AND category remains unchanged.
- Multi-location businesses where the business name changes across locations.

### NOT eligible for rebranding (requires new profile):
- If the name change does not meet the above criteria, it is considered a new business.
- MUST mark the existing profile as closed and create a new one.

### Ownership transfer:
- If assuming ownership of an existing profile, the previous owner must first add you as an owner and transfer ownership to you.

---

## 20. Prohibited and Restricted Content

### Fake and Misleading Content:

#### NOT ALLOWED:
- Content not based on a real experience or not accurately representing the location/product.
- Paid reviews or ratings (directly or in kind).
- Content posted from multiple accounts by or at the request of one person.
- Content posted using emulators, device tampering, modified operating systems, or methods to mimic genuine engagement.
- Soliciting or encouraging non-genuine content.
- Offering incentives (payment, discounts, free goods/services) in exchange for reviews, revision, or removal of negative reviews.
- Posting content on a competitor's profile to undermine their reputation.
- Reviews posted due to a conflict of interest (current/former employment, contractual relationship, professional/personal affiliations, industry competitors, familial relationships).
- Discouraging or prohibiting negative reviews.
- Selectively soliciting only positive reviews.
- Requiring or pressuring users to leave ratings/reviews while on premises.
- Requesting staff solicit a certain number of reviews or reviews with specific content (including staff member identification).

#### ALLOWED:
- Soliciting genuine reviews without offering incentives and without attempting to influence the rating or content.

### Impersonation:
- NOT ALLOWED: Using Google Maps to impersonate any person, group, or organization; content pretending to be a verified authoritative source.

### Misinformation:
- NOT ALLOWED: Harmful deceptive health/medical information; deceptive civic process information; manipulated content about newsworthy events.

### Misrepresentation:
- NOT ALLOWED: False/misleading descriptions of goods or services; distorting or omitting facts to scam users; content based on conflicts of interest; phishing, baiting, or tricking users.

### Harassment:
- NOT ALLOWED: Specific threats of harm; doxxing; unwanted sexualization or objectification.

### Hate Speech:
- NOT ALLOWED: Direct calls to action for violence against protected groups; dehumanizing, belittling, or vilifying content based on protected characteristics.

### Offensive Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: Attacking individuals/groups; deliberately provocative content; unsubstantiated allegations of unethical behavior or criminal wrongdoing.
- ALLOWED: Descriptions of negative experiences in a respectful manner.

### Personal Information:
- NOT ALLOWED: Distributing personal information without consent (full name, face in photos/videos, financial info, medical info, personal identification info).
- ALLOWED: Merchants posting their own business contact information; individual names if part of the commonly known business entity or for public-facing professionals.

### Obscenity and Profanity:
- NOT ALLOWED: Content using profanity to offend others or emphasize criticism.

### Sexually Explicit Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: Pornography, depiction of sexual acts/genitals/fetishes for sexual gratification, nude genitalia, bestiality content, content promoting sexually explicit behavior.

### Adult-Themed Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: Non-explicit pornography, content intended to be sexually gratifying, sexualization of inanimate objects, sexually suggestive poses.

### Violence and Gore:
- NOT ALLOWED: Violent/gory content involving people or animals; graphic violence; animal cruelty.

### Restricted Content:
- Content MUST NOT feature calls to action or offers for sale of regulated products/services (alcohol, gambling, tobacco, guns, health/medical devices, pharmaceuticals, adult services, financial services).
- ALLOWED: Incidental depictions (menu images, images where alcohol is present but not the main focus).

### Dangerous Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: Content facilitating serious harm to health/safety/property/animals/environment; promoting dangerous activities; instructional content for dangerous items.

### Child Safety:
- NOT ALLOWED: Content sexually exploiting children; predatory behavior content; depictions of abuse of minors; nudity of apparent minors.
- Violations will result in content removal and may be reported to authorities/law enforcement.

### Terrorist Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: Content inciting violence/promoting/celebrating terrorist acts; content by or on behalf of terrorist groups.

### Off-Topic Content:
- NOT ALLOWED: General political/social commentary; personal rants. Only post content based on experiences at the specific location.

### Advertising and Solicitation:
- NOT ALLOWED: Promotional/commercial content in reviews; posting email/phone/social media links/website URLs in reviews.

## 21. Third-Party Management Policies

### Definitions:
- **Third Party (3P):** An authorized agency managing business information on a Business Profile for a business they don't own.
- **End customers/clients:** Businesses who contract a third party to manage their GBP.

### Transparency Requirements:

#### REQUIRED:
- Communicate ALL changes and edits to end customers' profiles. Changes without consent are prohibited.
- Ensure end customers understand what GBP is and where their data is used.
- Proactively inform businesses about ownership and management options.
- End customers MUST retain ownership or co-ownership of their Business Profile at all times.
- Within 7 business days of termination notice, provide the client the ability to disassociate your Google Account and regain exclusive control.
- Relinquish and remove management permissions upon termination.
- If charging management fees: disclose in writing before managing the profile AND on customer invoices. Also disclose that Business Profile itself is a no-cost Google service.
- Avoid sudden or dramatic fee changes.
- Third parties serving small-to-medium businesses MUST share the "Working with a third party" disclosure notice with all customers; must have a link in a clearly discoverable location on their website and notify customers via email/mail.

### Insights/Reporting Requirements:
- MUST provide accurate GBP information and distinguish GBP data from other platform data.
- If reporting across platforms, GBP data MUST be reported separately (or also provided separately in an easy-to-access place alongside aggregated data).
- MUST NOT compare or share one customer's GBP data with other customers.
- If providing geographic performance data across platforms, MUST also separately provide the Google Business Profile location performance report.
- MUST share cost and performance reports in easily accessible ways.

### Prohibited Practices for Third Parties:

#### NOT ALLOWED:
- Claiming a business without the owner's express consent (consent must be written or a positive digital action; verbal consent is NOT sufficient).
- Preemptively claiming a profile to encourage/persuade/force a business to become a customer.
- Manipulating, impersonating, or gaining access to profiles without direct written authorization.
- Making false, misleading, or unrealistic claims, including:
  - Misrepresenting as Google through robocalls or other techniques.
  - Guaranteeing top placement on Google.
  - Claiming profiles will always appear in Search/Maps.
  - Representing free Google products as pay-for-insertion products.
  - Using misleading account names referencing Google or its entities.
- Harassing, abusive, or untrustworthy behavior:
  - Excessive cold calls without respecting do-not-call lists.
  - Pressuring customers to sign up or stay.
  - Threatening clients will lose their profile if they don't sign up.
  - Holding profiles hostage for money.
  - Phishing.
- Auto-reverting Google updates and suggested edits without consulting the merchant.
- Interfering with Google's monitoring or auditing of Business Profile activity.

### Account Setup Best Practices:
- Restrict owner/organization member roles to the most trusted employees.
- After creating a profile for a client: make the business owner the profile owner and yourself the manager.
- If a client already has a profile: ask to be invited as a manager, NOT as an owner.
- Do NOT share passwords with clients.
- If you no longer manage a profile, remove it from your account.

## 22. Product Approval Policies

### REQUIRED:
- Products MUST adhere to Google's Shopping Ads Policy.
- Products MUST meet product data specifications and data quality requirements.

### NOT ALLOWED:
- Content related to regulated products: alcohol, tobacco, gambling, financial services, pharmaceuticals and unapproved supplements, health/medical devices.

### Consequences:
- Non-compliant products may receive warnings, disapprovals, limited visibility, or suspension.
- Violating policies may result in removal of the entire product catalog.
- If a Business Profile is suspended, all products receive "Not Approved" status; they are automatically reprocessed within 5 days after reinstatement.

### Remediation:
- If a product is "Not Approved," click on it to see the reason and applicable policy.
- After editing, the product is automatically re-reviewed within 24 hours.
- A review can be requested if you've fixed the issue or disagree.

---

## 23. Account Restrictions and Suspensions

### Google Account not in good standing:
- If the Google Account is disabled, suspended, or deleted, the merchant must fix it before accessing their GBP.

### Account restricted from Business Profile activity:
- A pattern of policy violations may result in restricted access.
- All profiles associated with that account will be suspended.
- Content from a restricted account will be rejected.

### Identity and location changes:
- Edits that significantly change business identity WILL be rejected.
- Edits that significantly change business location WILL be rejected.

### Posting restrictions:
- When contributions to a profile are consistently unhelpful, harmful, off-topic, or violate policies, Google may limit or suspend user-generated content for that profile.

---

## 24. Marketing, Promotions, and Contests

### REQUIRED:
- Any promotion, marketing, contest, or giveaway MUST clearly link to the terms of the activity.
- MUST provide clear guidelines and qualifications.
- All promises, given or implied, MUST be adhered to.

---

## 25. Enforcement and Consequences

### Google reserves the right to:
- Suspend access to Business Profiles or other Google services for violations.
- Work with law enforcement if violations are unlawful.
- Remove non-compliant content.
- Contact third-party customers to notify them of violations.
- Restrict or suspend participation in Google third-party programs.
- Suspend Business Profiles and/or Google Accounts for serious policy violations.
- Permanently revoke the ability to manage Business Profiles for repeated or grave violations.
